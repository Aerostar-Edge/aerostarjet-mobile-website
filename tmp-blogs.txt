Everything You Need to Know About the Private Pilot	
License Course in India	
URL: aerostarjet.in/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-private-pilot-license-course-in-india/	
Getting your wings is one of the most exciting things a person can do. A Private Pilot License (PPL) is a	
qualification that permits the holder to act as a hobby pilot of an aircraft. PPL holders cannot be paid for	
their work but can fly for personal pleasure and recreation.	
Course Details	
ΓÇó Duration: 6 Months	
ΓÇó Flying Hours: 40 hours in Single Engine + 10 hours in Simulator (Total approx 50 hours)	
ΓÇó Solo Flying: Minimum 20 hours	
ΓÇó Aircraft: Cessna 172	
ΓÇó Eligibility: A pass in Class 10 STD	
ΓÇó Medical: Class II Medicals	
ΓÇó Language: The student should be proficient in English	
Ground Subjects (DGCA Examinations)	
ΓÇó Air Regulations ΓÇö Aerodrome Search & Rescue, Rules & Regulations, Customs & Health,	
Communication and Navigational Facilities.	
ΓÇó Air Navigation ΓÇö Theoretical and Practical Navigation.	
ΓÇó Aviation Meteorology ΓÇö Weather Codes & Plotting, Synoptic Meteorology, Weather Charts	 interpretation.	
ΓÇó Technical General (Aircraft and Engines) ΓÇö Design, Construction, Maintenance, Operation of Aircraft,	 Fuel, Loading, Weight Distribution.	
ΓÇó Specific Paper (Cessna-172) ΓÇö Limitations, Aircraft Engines, Systems, Weight & Balance	 Performance.	
Why Get a PPL?	
A PPL is the foundation for all further pilot training. Many students pursue a PPL as a stepping stone	
toward a Commercial Pilot License (CPL). Flying is also a valuable personal skill and hobby that opens	
up opportunities for air sports, private travel, and aviation enthusiast communities.
